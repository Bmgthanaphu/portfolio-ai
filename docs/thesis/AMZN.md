# AMZN Thesis
**Date:** 2026-05-21  
**Status:** Active  
**Price at thesis:** $265.01  
**Target hold:** 2-5 years  

## Why own it
1. **E-commerce Dominance:** Amazon continues to hold a significant market share in the global e-commerce sector, with a robust logistics network and a growing Prime membership base. This positions the company to capitalize on the ongoing shift toward online shopping, especially in emerging markets where internet penetration is increasing.

2. **AWS Growth Potential:** Amazon Web Services (AWS) remains a leader in the cloud computing space, with a diverse range of services and a strong customer base. The increasing demand for cloud solutions, driven by digital transformation across industries, provides substantial growth opportunities for AWS, contributing to Amazon's overall profitability.

3. **Diversification and Innovation:** Amazon's investments in various sectors, including advertising, healthcare, and entertainment, demonstrate its commitment to diversifying revenue streams. The company's focus on innovation, such as advancements in AI and logistics technology, positions it well for future growth and adaptability in a rapidly changing market.

## Key risks
1. **Regulatory Scrutiny:** Amazon faces increasing regulatory scrutiny in multiple jurisdictions, particularly concerning antitrust issues. Any significant legal actions or changes in regulations could impact its business operations and growth strategies.

2. **Competitive Pressures:** The e-commerce and cloud computing markets are highly competitive, with players like Walmart, Alibaba, and Microsoft aggressively pursuing market share. Amazon must continuously innovate and improve its offerings to maintain its competitive edge.

3. **Economic Sensitivity:** A downturn in the global economy could affect consumer spending and business investments, impacting Amazon's sales growth. Additionally, rising inflation and interest rates could squeeze margins and reduce discretionary spending.

## Kill conditions (EXIT if ANY of these happen)
- KC1: A significant decline in AWS revenue growth (e.g., below 10% year-over-year) indicating a loss of competitive advantage in the cloud market.
- KC2: Major regulatory actions resulting in substantial fines or operational restrictions that materially affect Amazon's business model.
- KC3: A sustained decline in e-commerce sales growth (e.g., below 5% year-over-year) indicating a loss of market share or consumer interest.

## Alert conditions (WATCH but don't panic)
- AC1: Changes in consumer behavior or spending patterns that could indicate a shift away from e-commerce, particularly in key markets.
- AC2: Developments in regulatory environments that could signal potential challenges or opportunities for Amazon's business operations.

## What to look for in earnings
- **AWS Revenue Growth Rate:** Monitoring the year-over-year growth rate of AWS to assess its competitive position and market demand.
- **E-commerce Sales Growth Rate:** Tracking the growth rate of e-commerce sales, particularly in key markets, to gauge overall consumer demand and market share.

## Notes
Amazon's ability to adapt to changing market conditions and consumer preferences will be crucial for its long-term success. Continuous monitoring of its competitive landscape, regulatory environment, and economic indicators will be essential to validate this investment thesis.
