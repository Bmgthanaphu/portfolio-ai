# VOO Thesis
**Date:** 2026-05-21  
**Status:** Active  
**Price at thesis:** $681.57  
**Target hold:** 2-5 years  

## Why own it
1. **Broad Market Exposure:** VOO, which tracks the S&P 500, provides exposure to a diversified portfolio of 500 of the largest U.S. companies. This diversification reduces individual stock risk and captures the overall growth of the U.S. economy, which has historically trended upwards over the long term.

2. **Low Expense Ratio:** VOO offers a very low expense ratio (0.03%), making it a cost-effective investment option. This allows investors to retain more of their returns over time compared to actively managed funds, which often have higher fees that can erode performance.

3. **Strong Historical Performance:** Historically, the S&P 500 has delivered an average annual return of around 10% over the long term. Given the current economic conditions and the potential for continued growth in sectors such as technology and healthcare, VOO is positioned to benefit from this trend.

## Key risks
1. **Economic Recession:** A significant downturn in the U.S. economy could negatively impact the performance of the S&P 500, leading to lower returns or potential losses in VOO.

2. **Interest Rate Increases:** Rising interest rates could lead to higher borrowing costs for companies, which may impact their profitability and, in turn, the overall performance of the index.

3. **Geopolitical Risks:** Events such as trade wars, political instability, or global conflicts could adversely affect U.S. companies and their stock prices, impacting VOO's performance.

## Kill conditions (EXIT if ANY of these happen)
- KC1: A sustained decline in GDP growth below 1% for two consecutive quarters, indicating a recession that could significantly impact the S&P 500.
- KC2: A significant increase in the Federal Funds Rate (e.g., above 5%) that leads to a marked decrease in corporate earnings across the index.
- KC3: A major geopolitical event (e.g., a military conflict involving the U.S. or significant trade sanctions) that leads to a prolonged market disruption and a fundamental shift in the economic landscape.

## Alert conditions (WATCH but don't panic)
- AC1: An increase in inflation rates above 4% that could lead to tighter monetary policy and impact economic growth.
- AC2: Significant changes in corporate earnings forecasts for the majority of S&P 500 companies, particularly in key sectors like technology and finance.

## What to look for in earnings
- **Earnings Growth Rate:** Monitor the year-over-year earnings growth rate of the S&P 500 companies to gauge overall economic health and corporate profitability.
- **Revenue Growth:** Look for consistent revenue growth across the index, as this indicates demand for goods and services and overall economic expansion.

## Notes
As a long-term investor, it is essential to remain focused on the fundamental strengths of VOO and the broader market rather than short-term price fluctuations. Continuous monitoring of economic indicators and corporate performance will be crucial in assessing the viability of holding VOO over the target period.
